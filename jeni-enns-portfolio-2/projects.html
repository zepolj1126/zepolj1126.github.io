Evercast Help Center redesign
June - September 2022

I initiated a complete redesign of a Zendesk Guide documentation website for users of a suite of applications for video conferencing and streaming.

Step 1: Analysis

I analyzed the existing website. Some of the major issues I found:

  • Overwhelming number of links and tiles

  • Hard for users to determine relevant content for their role and level of experience with the platform

  • Navigation pages had too many sections and articles, making it hard for users to quickly scan and find what’s relevant

  • Help topic pages had a black background and white text, making it hard to visually scan and read dense text

X
Step 2: Plan

I created a content strategy of how to fix the issues and move forward. In my plan, I:

  • Created a new information architecture in Miro, organizing the topics according to the user’s role and goals

  • Created a wire frame in Figma for the new structure of the website

  • Navigation pages had too many sections and articles, making it hard for users to quickly scan and find what’s relevant

  • Presented my findings and the future state of the website to company leadership for their feedback and sign-off

Step 3: Implement

I collaborated with the development, support, and UX design teams to implement the website redesign. The result:

  • Home page has six main categories of information according to the user’s goals and roles

  • Navigation pages are broken down into smaller sections to help guide users

  • Help topic pages have a light background and black text, making them easier to read

  • Expandable/collapsible accordion sections so users can read only content that’s relevant

  • Navigation was moved to the left on topic pages

Evercast UI text overhaul
May - August 2022

I collaborated with the UI/UX designer to overhaul all UI texts across the platform to make them more concise, clear, consistent, and aligned with the company style standard.

Steps

Here’s how we approached the project:

  1. We took screen captures of every screen across all of the applications

  2. We organized them in Figma so we could easily get an overview of them

  3. We reviewed them together and made comments on the various images of where text changes needed to be made

  4. In Confluence, I transferred the images that required changes and made tables with before and after texts

  5. In Jira, I made task cards for the developers that included the changes to be made
